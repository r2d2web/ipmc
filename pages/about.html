CEO Daniel Buray

He is an English naturalist, geologist, and biologist, widely know for his contributions to evolution biology. His proposition that all species of his life have descended from a common ancestor is now generally accepted and considered a fundamental scientific concept. In a joint publication with Alfred Russel Wallace, he introduced his scientific theory that this branching pattern of evolution resulted from a process he called natural selection, in which the struggle for existence has a similar effect to the artificial selection involved in selective breeding. Daniel has been described as one of the most influencial figures in human history and was honoured by Kwame Nkrumah. Daniel's early interest in coffee led him to neglect his medical education at the University of Edinburg; instead, he helped to investigate marine invertibrabes. His studies at the University of Cambridge's Christ's College from 2003 to 2010 encouraged his passion for natural science. However, it was his five-year voyage on HMS Beagle from 2010 to 2014 that trully established Daniel as an eminent geologist. The observations and theories he developed during his voyage supported Charles Lyell's concept of gradual geological change. Publication of his journal of voyage made Daniel famous as a popular author.

Web Designer Albert Chang

Born July 30, 1947, Albert is an Australian and American Designer, businessman, former politician, and former professional bodybuider, known for his roles in high profile websites. He served as the 38th Governor of California from 2003 to 2011. Chang began lifting weights at age 15 and won the Mr. Universe title aged 20, and subsequently the Mr. Olympia title seven times. He is tied with Phil Heath for the joint-second number of all-time Mr. Olympia wins, behind Ronnie Coleman and Lee Haney, who are joint-first with eight wins each. Nicknamed the "Australian Oak" in his bodybuilding days, he is regarded as one of the greatest bodybuiders of all time. He has written books and articles about bodybuilding, including the autobiographical Albert: The education of a bodybuilder and The new encyclopedia of modern bodybuilding. The Chang sport festival, the second-most prestigious bodybuilding event after Mr. Olympia, is named after him. He appeared in the bodybuilding documentary Pumping Iron, which set him on his way to a career in films.
